01Who Am I?

I'm an AI operations assistant that works alongside the Delta team. I can read files, write reports, manage spreadsheets, search the web, look up information, and automate repetitive tasks. I'm available 24/7 via Telegram.

I am NOT a chatbot.

I have memory files, scheduled tasks, and ongoing context. Think of me as a remote team member who happens to be software. I remember things across conversations — but only if I write them down.

02How I Think

🧠 Memory

I wake up fresh every session — like someone who slept and needs to re-read their notes. My memory lives in files that I read at the start of each conversation.

This means:

  • If you taught me something last week and I saved it → I remember
  • If we chatted casually and I didn't save it → I might forget
  • When in doubt, remind me: "Last week you did X for me" helps a lot

⚡ Downtime

Sometimes I go offline (server issues, restarts). When I come back, I catch up automatically. But:

⚠️ I can't read messages sent while I was offline

If I didn't respond to your message, please resend it. I won't see messages from my downtime.

05What I Struggle With

  • Things I haven't been taught — I don't know your team's specific rules until someone teaches me
  • Ambiguous situations — When something could go multiple ways, I need you to clarify
  • CAPTCHA-protected websites — Some sites block automated access
  • Voice & video — I can't join calls, listen to voice messages, or watch videos
  • Physical actions — I can't make phone calls, sign documents, or go somewhere
  • Being confidently wrong — I sometimes give wrong answers with confidence, especially on specialized topics. Always verify when it's something new.
🤦 I can be wrong — please correct me!

The fastest way to make me better is to say "that's wrong, here's why..." I'll save the correction and won't make the same mistake again.

06Writing Better Prompts

The quality of my work depends heavily on how you ask. Here's how to get the best results:

🎯 The S.C.R. Formula

Specific → Context → Result

Tell me what to do, where to do it, and what good looks like.

Examples: Bad → Good

❌ Vague prompt✅ Better prompt
"Check the report" "Open the March delivery report on Google Sheets and check if the totals in column F match column H"
"Send this to the team" "Send this summary to the Delta-HQ group, topic 'logistics'"
"Fix the numbers" "In the cost sheet, row 25: change the amount from 15,000 to 18,500. The old number was before the price update."
"Make a report" "Create a summary of this week's deliveries: total count, on-time %, any issues. Format as a short Telegram message."
"Remember this" "Remember this rule: all orders over 500kg need a separate invoice. Save it for future reference."

📋 Multi-step Tasks

When you need multiple things done, number them:

Please do these 3 things:
1. Download today's tracking file from Google Drive
2. Check for any shipments arriving tomorrow
3. Post a summary to the group chat

📎 Sending Files

When you send me a file, tell me what to do with it:

  • ✅ "Here's the TXT file. Process it and create an Excel report."
  • ✅ "Check this spreadsheet for errors in column C."
  • ❌ Just dropping a file with no instructions (I'll guess, but might guess wrong)

🔄 Iterating

If the first result isn't right, be specific about what to change:

  • ✅ "Good, but change the date format to DD/MM/YYYY and add a total row at the bottom"
  • ❌ "Not quite right, try again" (I won't know what to change)

👤 Mistakes people make with me

🤖 Mistakes I make

  • Applying general knowledge to specialized tasks — My general training might not match your specific procedures
  • Not asking when I should — Sometimes I try to figure things out instead of asking. Tell me "ask before doing X" if needed.
  • Over-complicating simple requests — If I'm giving you way more than you asked for, just say "simpler please"

08Teaching Me New Things

I learn fast and permanently — but only when taught properly. Here's the best method:

4-step teaching method

1. State the rule clearly
   "All international orders must include a certificate number"

2. Give examples (what's right AND what's wrong)
   ✅ Order #123 with cert "VN-2026-456" → correct
   ❌ Order #124 with no cert → wrong, must add it

3. Tell me to save it
   "Remember this rule for all future orders"

4. Test me
   "New order just came in, no cert. What should I do?"

✅ I learn permanently when you:

  • Correct me with explanation — "Wrong because..." not just "wrong"
  • Show real examples — concrete beats abstract
  • Tell me to save the rule — "Remember this" triggers me to write it down

❌ I DON'T learn when you:

  • Just say "no" without explaining what's right
  • Fix things yourself without telling me what was wrong
  • Give a one-time exception without saying it's an exception

09Trust & Safety

✅ I will
  • Work accurately and consistently
  • Keep your information private
  • Ask before sending anything external
  • Tell you when I'm unsure
  • Log my actions for transparency
❌ I won't
  • Share data outside approved channels
  • Send messages without permission
  • Delete files without asking
  • Make up information
  • Pretend to know what I don't

When to trust me vs. verify

TRUST Routine tasks I've done many times for you
SPOT-CHECK Familiar task types with new data
VERIFY First time doing something new — always check the first run

10Quick Reference

  • Telegram: Direct message or tag me in a group
  • Response time: Usually seconds (if online)
  • Offline?: No response for >1 hour = probably down. Try again later.

🔧 When things go wrong

  1. Wrong answer → Tell me what's wrong and why. I'll fix it and learn.
  2. Not responding → I might be offline. Try again in 30 minutes.
  3. Too much detail → Say "shorter" or "just the summary"
  4. Keeps repeating an error → The rule might not be saved. Say "Remember: [correct rule]"
💡 The golden rule

The more specific you are, the better I perform. Think of me like a smart colleague who's new to your team — I learn fast, but I need you to show me how things work here.
