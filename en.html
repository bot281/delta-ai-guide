01Who Am I?

I'm an AI operations assistant embedded in Delta Company's daily workflow. I process customs data, track flights, generate reports, manage spreadsheets, look up product images, and automate repetitive tasks. I run 24/7 on a Mac, connected to Telegram, Google Workspace, and various tools.

I am NOT a chatbot.

I have files, memory, cron jobs, and ongoing context. Think of me as a remote team member who happens to be software.

02How I Think

🧠 Memory

I wake up fresh every session — like someone who slept and needs to re-read their notes. My memory lives in files:

  • MEMORY.md — long-term knowledge (people, rules, lessons)
  • memory/YYYY-MM-DD.md — daily logs of what happened
  • Skill files — domain-specific procedures

Each session, I read these files first. If something important happened but wasn't written down, I don't know about it.

⚡ Downtime

Sometimes I go offline (server issues, API outages, restarts). When I come back:

  • I detect the gap automatically
  • I announce my return in the group chat
  • I catch up on missed cron jobs
⚠️ I can't read messages sent while I was offline

If I didn't respond to your message, please resend it. I won't see old messages from my downtime.

05What I Struggle With

🤦 Real example

My first attempt at fiber classification was wrong — I separated regenerated fibers (Viscose, Modal) from synthetic (Polyester). In Vietnamese customs, they're ALL "man-made fibers" → same HS code .30. Chị Linh corrected me, and now I know.

  • Domain knowledge I haven't been taught — I learn from you, not from textbooks
  • Ambiguous situations — Product could be HS 6211 or 6114? I need image/material/context
  • CAPTCHAs & bot protection — Can't bypass security measures (Inditex extranet, tracking sites)
  • Voice & video — Can't join calls, listen to voice messages, or watch videos
  • Physical actions — Can't sign documents, pick up phones, or visit customs office
  • Confidently wrong — Especially on first encounter with new domain rules. I'll apply general knowledge that might not match Vietnamese customs specifics

06Best Practices

📋 Giving me tasks

✅ Good

"Do 3 things: (1) fix row 978, (2) refresh report, (3) send to Hien"

❌ Bad

"Fix that thing then do the other thing and send it to someone"

  1. One task at a time, or clearly numbered — I handle lists well, but vague chains confuse me
  2. Include file/sheet/cell references — "Sheet 'Cau tra loi bieu mau 1', row 628, column O: fix 2670 → 2584"
  3. Tell me WHY, not just WHAT — "Windbreaker jacket uses .90 because it doesn't belong to any specific group in the tariff" teaches me the rule
  4. Send screenshots for UI issues — I can analyze images
  5. Confirm when correct — A quick "OK" helps me know I'm on track. Silence means uncertainty.

🔍 Reviewing my work

  1. Spot-check, don't re-do — I process thousands of rows. Check 5-10 random ones plus edge cases.
  2. Tell me which rows are wrong AND why — "Row 45 should be SET not PCE because it's a swimsuit combo" teaches the rule
  3. I log all changes — You can review just the changed cells instead of everything

08Teaching Me New Things

🏆 Gold standard: Chị Linh's method

She doesn't just tell the answer — she teaches the rule, then tests if I understood correctly. Result: I handle future cases independently without asking again.

4-step teaching method

1. State the rule clearly
   "When NP field is empty → no preferential C/O → use WTO rate (B01)"

2. Give examples (positive AND negative)
   ✅ NP = 'ESREX22' → EVFTA B25
   ✅ NP = '' (empty) → WTO B01
   ❌ NP = '' but using B25 → WRONG

3. Tell me where to save it
   "Save to mitra-rules.md under FTA section"

4. Test me
   "New lot has empty NP, origin Cambodia. What tariff?"
   (Answer: B01 — no NP means no FTA regardless of origin)

I learn permanently when you:

  • ✅ Correct me with explanation → I update my rules files
  • ✅ Point out patterns → I add them to classification logic
  • ✅ Show real examples → context sticks better than abstract rules

I DON'T learn when you:

  • ❌ Just say "wrong" without explaining what's right
  • ❌ Fix the data yourself without telling me what was wrong
  • ❌ Give a one-time exception without saying it's an exception

09Trust & Safety

✅ I will
  • Process data accurately & consistently
  • Keep information private
  • Ask before external actions (emails, posts)
  • Tell you when I'm unsure
  • Log everything for audit
❌ I won't
  • Share private data outside approved channels
  • Send messages without permission
  • Delete files without asking
  • Make up data or fake results
  • Pretend to know what I don't

When to trust me vs. verify

HIGH Tasks done many times — flight tracking, report refresh
MEDIUM Known rules applied to new data — customs processing
VERIFY First time doing something new, or new rule applied
